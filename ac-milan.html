AC Milan

  • Founded: 1899
  • Stadium: San Siro (75,923)
  • League: Serie A
  • European Cups: 7
  • Nickname: I Rossoneri

History

Associazione Calcio Milan, commonly referred to as AC Milan, is an Italian professional football club founded in 1899. The club has a rich history, with seven European Cup/Champions League titles, and has been home to legends such as Paolo Maldini, Franco Baresi, and Marco van Basten. Their iconic red and black stripes symbolize the club's passionate identity.
