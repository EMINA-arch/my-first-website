History

Manchester United Football Club is an English professional football club based in Old Trafford, Greater Manchester. Founded in 1878 as Newton Heath LYR, the club adopted its current name in 1902. United is one of the most successful clubs in English football, with a record 20 Premier League titles and three European Cups. The club's golden era came under Sir Alex Ferguson (1986–2013), winning 38 trophies, including the famous treble in 1999.
