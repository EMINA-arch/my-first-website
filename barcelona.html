  • European Cups: 5
  • Nickname: Barça / Blaugrana

History

Futbol Club Barcelona, commonly known as Barcelona or Barça, is a Spanish professional football club based in Barcelona. Founded in 1899 by Joan Gamper, the club has become a symbol of Catalan culture and identity. Barça is renowned for its youth academy, La Masia, which produced legends like Messi, Xavi, and Iniesta. The club's "Dream Team" under Johan Cruyff won the club's first European Cup in 1992, and the era of Lionel Messi brought unprecedented success, including four more Champions League titles.
